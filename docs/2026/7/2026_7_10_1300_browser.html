Factory fire kills at least 28 in China's 'shoe capital' 中国“鞋都”工厂火灾造成至少28人死亡

📅 2026-07-10 13:00 🔗 阅读原文 🔙 返回日历

A fire at a shoe factory in the south-eastern Chinese city of Jinjiang has killed at least 28 people, according to the state news agency.据国家通讯社报道，中国东南部城市晋江一家鞋厂发生火灾，已造成至少 28 人死亡。

Dramatic footage posted by Xinhua showed huge plumes of black smoke rising from a building as well as people who appeared to be trapped on the roof.新华社发布的戏剧性画面显示，一座建筑物冒出巨大的黑烟，人们似乎被困在屋顶上。

President Xi Jinping said the blaze had caused "major casualties", adding those responsible must be held "strictly accountable".习近平主席表示，火灾造成“重大人员伤亡”，并补充说必须“严肃追究责任者”。

Jinjiang, in the province of Fujian, is often referred to as China's "shoe capital" as it reportedly manufactures 20% of the world's sports shoes. 福建省晋江经常被称为中国的“鞋都”，因为据报道，该市生产了全球 20% 的运动鞋。

The blaze erupted at the Huiteng Footwear factory at around noon local time (05:00 BST) on Thursday. 当地时间周四中午（英国夏令时 05:00）左右，辉腾鞋业工厂发生火灾。

Hundreds of rescue workers were rushed to the scene, state broadcaster CCTV reports, and more than 200 people were evacuated.据国家电视台中央电视台报道，数百名救援人员被送往现场，200多人被疏散。

It is unclear how many have been injured.目前尚不清楚有多少人受伤。

Authorities dispatched more than 500 personnel to extinguish the blaze and rescue people, state media reported.据官方媒体报道，当局出动了 500 多名人员扑灭大火并营救人员。

State media also reported that there were nearly 240 people at the factory when the fire broke out.官方媒体还报道称，火灾发生时工厂内有近240人。

Among the 213 people who were evacuated, two of them later died in hospital. Twenty-six others, who had been initially missing, were found dead.被疏散的213人中，有两人后来在医院死亡。最初失踪的另外 26 人被发现死亡。

Initial reports suggest the fire may have started on the factory's ground floor where flammable materials were stored.初步报告显示，火灾可能是从工厂存放易燃材料的底层开始的。

Authorities have detained a number of people who work for the factory's owners, and the firm's bank account was frozen, according to state media.

China started a campaign to prevent fires in high-rise buildings after a blaze ripped through several Hong Kong apartment buildings in November, killing 168 people.

Xi said Chinese officials must draw "profound lessons" from "several major industrial safety accidents" in the country this year.

He said "rigorous and effective safety measures" must be implemented.
